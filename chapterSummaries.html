Chapter Summaries

  • Chapters 1-2
  • The story begins in the Time Traveller's home as he speaks to a group of men, including the narrator
  • He tells of the fourth dimension and uses the example of how a cube exists in time AND space, as time is the fourth dimension
  • The men are not sure whether to believe him, as he goes even further saying that we should be able to speed up, slow down, or even go backwards in the fourth dimension, just as we can in the other three
  • He then builds a small time machine out of ivory and crystal, its about the size of a clock
  • He has a guest send the machine forward in time with a lever, and it disappears, amazing the men
  • He then shows them a larger machine in which he plans to explore time
  • The narrator tells that not many of the men believed the Time Traveller as he is very intelligent and often played pranks of elaborate proportions
  • A week later the narrator returns to the Time Traveller's home for another dinner, this time with some fewer guests of the previous week, and some new guests
  • The Time Traveller has not appeared yet to the guests but when he does he is dusty and disheveled
  • He washes up and the narrator suggests that he has perhaps traveled through time, however many men do not believe him and when the Traveller returns to tell his story many men object as they don't believe he could travel through time
  • He tells them they must not interrupt him and they agree all listening with there attention growing as the story begins
  • Chapters 3-4
  • The Traveller tells how he went to his machine and slightly pushed the lever forward, after feeling a dizzying sensation, he emerges seeing that the clock in his lab has advanced 5 hours
  • He then decides to push the machine a bit more, traveling faster and faster through time, his surroundings change and he fears he might eventually travel to a time where the machine is impeded by an object so he quickly stops it and is thrown through the air
  • He has stepped out into a hail storm that shortly subsides, he then notices a giant statue of a white sphinx on a large bronze pedestal
  • Fearful that the future has evolved into an age of cruel and savage man, he slowly peers around
  • He notices many large buildings, and as he turns his machine right-side up he is noticed by figures dressed in rich robes by a near building
  • A creature approaches him, appearing very beautiful, yet frail as if it were suffering from tuberculosis
  • More creatures approach him surrounding him and speaking in a very smooth and sweet accent, he takes out the control device from his machine so as it won't be used by anyone else
  • He describes the creatures as having larger than normal eyes, curly hair, and very thin red lips, he then points up to the sky trying to explain how he came there
  • The creatures do not seem to understand him and he thinks that perhaps they are not as bright as he imagined people from the future to be
  • They then take the Traveller to a large building covered in hieroglyphics, they also feed him strange fruit, and try to teach him a few words
  • He decides to explore there world, it is currently 802,701 AD, he notices much of it is in ruins, and that all the creatures live in large buildings together
  • He explains that there is no gender distinction, and that old people seem to not exist, that it seems to be a communist paradise, and all hardship and fear has been eliminated
  • He thinks that perhaps over centuries the effort to make life simpler has made it so simple that there is no danger, hard ship, or need for families
  • He then states that his initial beliefs were all wrong
  • Chapter 5
  • As night falls he heads back to his machine, however he is surprised to find it missing
  • It has been disabled so it couldn't have been moved in time, and the people seemed to frail to move it, which causes him to panic as he has no idea where it is
  • He runs around the sphinx searching for it but only ends up startling a white creature, he then runs inside the building demanding his time machine, startling the creatures
  • He eventually calms down and tries to deduce what could have happened
  • He believes it couldn't have been moved very far, and that perhaps it is hidden in the base of the sphinx
  • After failing to open the base and upsetting the creatures in the process he decides to be patient and take time to get to know the area
  • He notices strange wells all over the place that seem to be sucking in air, he also changes his mind about the civilization being decadent and automated
  • It seems that the clothes they were must come from somewhere and there are only large buildings
  • While exploring the Traveller sees a creature drowning in the river, Weena, and he manages to save her, causing her to become affectionate toward him
  • She greets him later when he returns to the white sphinx statue, and seems to latch onto him
  • The creatures are afraid of the dark and sleep in large groups in the buildings, Weena makes the Traveller sleep with the groups
  • On the fourth morning he awakes and enters an old ruin, seeing two large eyes staring at him, and it appears as if it is a white ape-like creature
  • The animal flees and he fails to track it down
  • He later deduces that the new creature must live underground, and that perhaps the wells are there to supply a whole race of underground creatures, perhaps the laborers of the creatures above ground
  • He later learns that his belief is correct and that underground creatures called "Morlocks" live and the creatures on the surface are called "Eloi"
  • He asks Weena more questions about the Morlocks, but causes her to become upset
  • Chapters 6-7
  • The Traveller believes he has to enter the Morlocks world in order to find his machine
  • Off in the distance he sees something he describes as the Palace of Green Porcelain, but he decides to not approach it and instead descend down a well
  • He makes his way down a well which seems to be very deep, he manages to take rest on a small alcove but is awoken by the touch of a Morlock
  • He lights a match and sees a few of them as they scramble away from him, using matches he makes his way around investigating and seeing large chambers filled with creatures
  • He notices large machines pumping in air, and also it appears they are eating some sort of meat
  • His matches eventually run out and the Morlocks try to capture him, yet he manages to escape up a well just in time
  • He decides he must come up with a new plan of attack, and also comes to think the meat the Morlocks are eating is actually Eloi that are hunted at night, explaining why the Eloi are afraid of the dark
  • The Traveller comes to believe that the Morlocks, once sent underground by the Eloi, have now gained power over the Eloi, and that they have settled on being cannibals in order to survive
  • He is nervous and tries to find a place to sleep for the night, he believes he has upset the Morlocks
  • The decision is made to head to the Palace of Green Porcelain and he travels with Weena who spends some time resting on his shoulders, she also supplies him with flowers which he shows to his guests
  • The journey is long and they do not reach there destination before nightfall, instead deciding to stop on the border of a forest, The Traveller keeps watch as Weena sleeps
  • Chapters 8-10
  • They reach the Palace of Green Porcelain, which in fact is made out of Green Porcelain, and is a decrepit museum
  • There is a chemistry exhibit and the Traveller manages to salvage camphor, which was often used in torches
  • He finds a book of matches which is very helpful as he has run out and that keeps the Morlocks away
  • He explores the museum and sees many old books and a machinery hall, Weena is frightened by the museum
  • There is a dark hallway and they hear the sound of Morlocks so they flee after the Traveller breaks a lever off of a machine
  • He sets a fire to scare of Morlocks that follow them out of the museum and then escapes into the forest with Weena
  • While in the forest more Morlocks approach but the Traveller starts another small fire again scaring them away and he takes this time to rest feeling safe
  • The Traveller is awoken by Morlocks grabbing him so he grabs the lever and swings, killing a few and forcing the rest to flee
  • He looks for Weena but she is no where to be seen, and he then realizes that he has started a forest fire with the first fire he set
  • The Traveller finds the way out of the forest by following the Morlocks, he eventually comes to a large hill and clearing where a group of blinded, and confused Morlocks congregate
  • He gathers himself on top of the hill and then heads back to the sphinx and pedestal
  • He arrives back at the pedestal and sees it is open and behind the wall is his time machine, he walks inside and the doors are shut behind him, he is ambushed by the Morlocks
  • He manages to quickly get to the machine and escapes to the future just in time
  • Chapter 11-12 and Epilogue
  • He travels very fast into the future and eventually stops noticing the sun is dying the moon does not circle the earth anymore, and the planet is dying
  • While out of the machine he notices very large creatures, giant crabs, and butterflies, he is also now on a beach
  • He then travels even further into the future and the earth seems to be coming to an end, there is not much sign of life, only a lichen on the beach, (he's thirty million years in the future)
  • He steps out and the air is thin and all signs point to the end of the earth, there is only one sign of animal life, a large black creature in the distance with tentacles
  • He becomes light headed at the lack of air and travels back in time slowly gaining his health back
  • He travels all the way back to his laboratory where he steps out just in time for the dinner meeting
  • The group is amazed by his story but is still very skeptical
  • The Traveller seems to be overwhelmed as if he forgot whether what just happened was real, and he rushes to the machine to see it covered in grass and dirt
  • The guests leave and the narrator returns the next day to speak to the Time Traveller
  • He finds him preparing for another journey in the future, so the Time Traveller tells him to wait a half hour and he will be back
  • He doesn't return however and it has been three years since he left
  • The only evidence left of the time travel are two brittle alien flowers
  • The flowers are living proof that human tenderness still lives on despite the fact strength and mind have decayed
